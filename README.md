### 📄 Abstract
Low-power operational amplifier design in scaled CMOS requires reconciling gain, bandwidth, stability, and energy efficiency parameters that frequently conflict. This review compares five recent IEEE-published works (45 nm, 90 nm, and 180 nm nodes) targeting portable biomedical and IoT applications.

Key techniques analyzed:
- Bulk-driven differential pairs (±0.5 V operation)
- Moderate-inversion biasing with dynamic tail-current boost
- Two-stage topologies with nulling-resistor & indirect current-buffer compensation
- Power gating for ultra-low standby leakage (down to 50.36 pW)

### 📊 Key Performance Highlights
- Open-loop gain: **57.85 dB – 72.4 dB**
- Unity-gain bandwidth: **2.31 MHz – 50.25 MHz**
- Active power: as low as **3.76 µW**
- Best phase margin: **86.3°** (current-buffer compensation)
- Standby power: **50.36 pW** (259× reduction via power gating)

### 🔍 Research Gaps & Future Directions
- Missing: Noise characterisation, PVT analysis, and silicon validation
- Proposed: Hybrid bulk-driven + power-gated + current-buffer architectures for implantable biomedical devices
